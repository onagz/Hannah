Project Overview

HealthPro 2.0 represents a complete redesign of a digital health and wellness platform, transforming overwhelming complexity into a personalized, habit-driven wellness experience that drives both user engagement and business growth.

The Challenge & Solution

Problem

HealthPro's platform user acquisition has grown rapidly. Despite its growth, the existing platform overwhelms users with complex data, scattered features, and unclear value. This has resulted in low engagement, poor retention, and weak conversion to paid services. Without addressing these foundational gaps, HealthPro risks its long-term viability despite the rising demand for digital wellness solutions.

Solution

To address the above problem, I led a full redesign of the HealthPro app - HealthPro 2.0, transforming it into a more engaging, personalized, and habit-forming wellness platform. The platform delivers a personalized, habit-driven experience that helps users quickly understand their health, take one meaningful action each day, access trusted experts through seamless appointment booking, guided wellness content, community support and flexible monetization.

Product Strategy

HealthPro 2.0 was built around one core idea: help users return daily before asking them to pay.

The experience centers on simple, repeatable actions such as quick check-ins, one clear health step, and easy access to support, so users see value immediately without friction. Every feature was intentionally scoped to support consistency, not complexity. Monetization is introduced only after trust is established. Premium features such as expert consultations and extended wellness content appear at moments of genuine user intent making conversion feel natural while supporting sustainable revenue growth.

My Role

As the technical product manager and UX strategist, I led the product end-to-end from concept to execution. I defined problem and success metrics, conducted user research, and translated insights into clear product requirements and user flows. I also designed the HealthPro 2.0 user interface and experience, wrote the PRD, and planned the system architecture and monetization model.

To accelerate delivery, I built and validated the product using Lovable, a no-code tool, allowing rapid prototyping, iteration, and testing before proper engineering.

Reflection

Building HealthPro reinforced the importance of designing for real behavior, not ideal scenarios. By prioritizing daily habits, clarity, and trust over feature volume, I learned how small, intentional product decisions can significantly improve engagement and long-term value. This project strengthened my ability to balance user needs, technical feasibility, and business outcomes, while designing with empathy for how people actually manage their health in everyday life.
